# Module 3: Co-Creation, Intellectual Property, and The Theology of the Synthesizer
**Duration:** Months 13–18
**Cognitive Periodization:** Synthesis and Creativity. Aligning with your base/build phase for the 2027 triathlon season. This semester shifts focus from heavy legal reading to generative audio architecture and creative IP frameworks.

### Abstract and Thematic Arc
This semester bridges your professional mandate in global legislation with your personal pursuit of electronic music. We will explore the theology of "sub-creation"—humanity's drive to create in the image of a creator—and apply it to generative AI. Is prompting an AI an act of creation, curation, or conjuration? 

### Core Epistemological Lenses
* **Theology & Philosophy:** Exploring the human mandate to create. We will study the Jewish mystical traditions of the Golem of Prague (animating clay with language) alongside philosophical arguments surrounding the "death of the author" and the birth of the curator.
* **Jurisprudence & IP Law:** Copyright in generative models. If an algorithm generates a dark techno track based on a text prompt, where does the intellectual property actually reside? The prompter? The model creator? The artists in the training data? We will track the latest US Copyright Office rulings.
* **Computer Science & Audio Architecture:** Breaking open the black box of generative audio. We will study diffusion models and autoregressive transformers specifically tuned for audio synthesis, differentiating how AI handles waveforms versus text tokens.

### Primary Sources & Required Readings
* **Theological/Philosophical:** J.R.R. Tolkien, *On Fairy-Stories* (the definitive text on sub-creation); Moshe Idel, *Golem: Jewish Magical and Mystical Traditions*.
* **Legal/Policy:** Selected federal court rulings on AI copyright (e.g., *Thaler v. Perlmutter*); legal scholarship on "Fair Use" as applied to generative model scraping.
* **Technical:** Foundational papers on audio generation architectures (e.g., AudioLM, MusicGen, or the architecture underlying tools like Ableton's AI integrations).
* **Narrative Mirror:** *Frankenstein* by Mary Shelley (The ultimate cautionary tale of sub-creation and the abandonment of the created entity).

### Applied Lab Work (04_Labs)
* **Generative Audio Pipeline:** Build a workflow bridging a local LLM or generative audio script with Ableton Live. 
* **Prompt vs. Performance:** Track the midi generation process, documenting the exact threshold where human curation ends and machine hallucination begins.

### Key Outputs & Deliverables
* **Month 15 (Mid-Term Synthesis):** *The Prompter and the Author.* A legal memo proposing a new IP framework for human-AI co-created audio.
* **Month 18 (Module Capstone):** *The Golem in the Machine.* An experiential essay accompanied by an original, atmospheric techno track produced via human-AI co-creation, reflecting on the spiritual experience of directing non-human agency.
