# Module 1: The Architecture of Mind & Pluralistic Personhood

**Duration:** Months 1–6

## Focus Areas
This module investigates the intersections between ancient philosophical/theological frameworks and modern machine learning architectures. It explores how models of the mind from Judaism and Buddhism might inform our understanding of alignment and the structure of Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs).

- **Theology & Philosophy:** Jewish conceptions of the soul, Buddhist models of consciousness (e.g., *Anatta* or non-self).
- **Computer Science:** Fundamentals of alignment, the architecture of Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs), and artificial neural networks.
- **Synthesis:** How can ancient understandings of pluralistic personhood inform the way we align and interpret the behavior of complex neural architectures?

## Key Outputs

1. **Month 3:** Short Synthesis paper connecting theological models of the mind with early neural network theory.
2. **Month 6:** *"The Ghost in the Code"* essay — a deep dive into alignment, pluralistic personhood, and the emergent properties of MLPs.
