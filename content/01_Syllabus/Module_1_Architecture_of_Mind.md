# Module 1: The Architecture of Mind, Pluralistic Personhood, and Distributed Agency
**Duration:** Months 1–6
**Cognitive Periodization:** Heavy theoretical reading and primary source analysis in Months 1–3; shifting to synthesis, audio-lectures, and technical lab execution in Months 4–6 to accommodate peak endurance training blocks.

### Abstract and Thematic Arc
This module elevates "alignment" from a purely mathematical optimization problem to an interdisciplinary inquiry. By investigating the structural parallels between ancient theological frameworks of the soul and the architecture of modern artificial neural networks, we will establish a theoretical baseline for algorithmic personhood. If consciousness or agency is not a singular monolithic entity, but a distributed network of competing components, traditional legislative and ethical frameworks must be entirely rewired.

### Core Epistemological Lenses
* **Theology & Philosophy:** Analyzing the Jewish tripartite model of the soul (*Nefesh*, *Ruach*, *Neshamah*) alongside the historical concept of the Golem (sub-creation). Contrasting this with the Buddhist doctrine of *Anatta* (non-self) and the Five Skandhas (aggregates), exploring how emergent identity arises from decentralized parts.
* **Computer Science & Mechanistic Interpretability:** Moving beyond basic Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) to study Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architectures and attention mechanisms. How do sub-networks route decisions, and how does this structural pluralism mirror ancient models of the human mind?
* **Jurisprudence & Tech Policy:** The collapse of singular culpability. If an AI acts through a decentralized, multi-agent architecture, how do current international legal frameworks assign liability? We will map the legal precedent of "corporate personhood" onto algorithmic entities.

### Primary Sources & Required Readings
* **Theological Foundations:** Selections from *The Guide for the Perplexed* (Maimonides); Selections from the *Abhidharmakośa* (Vasubandhu) on the aggregation of consciousness.
* **Technical/Alignment:** *Mechanistic Interpretability and the Math of Truth* (Frontier AI safety literature); *Attention Is All You Need* (Vaswani et al., 2017).
* **Legal/Policy:** Selected case law and academic literature on corporate vs. algorithmic sovereignty; early drafts of autonomous agent liability frameworks.
* **Narrative Mirror:** *Blindsight* by Peter Watts (A hard sci-fi exploration of hyper-intelligence existing entirely without human-like consciousness or singular identity).

### Applied Lab Work (04_Labs)
* **Architectural Reconstruction:** Implement basic MLPs and a rudimentary Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) routing gate from scratch in PyTorch.
* **Interpretability Sandbox:** Explore toy alignment models to visualize feature superposition and how abstract "concepts" are distributed across hidden layers rather than localized in single neurons.

### Key Outputs & Deliverables
* **Month 3 (Mid-Term Synthesis):** *The Aggregated Agent.* A 3,000-word paper cross-examining the legal concept of the "corporation as a person" with the Buddhist concept of the Skandhas, applied directly to MoE architectures.
* **Month 6 (Module Capstone):** *The Ghost in the Code: Liability in Pluralistic Systems.* A comprehensive essay serving as an M.Phil dissertation primer. It must reconcile the theological architecture of the mind with actionable policy frameworks for misaligned neural networks.
