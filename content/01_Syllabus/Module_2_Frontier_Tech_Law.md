# Module 2: Frontier Tech Law, Cryptographic Sovereignty, and The Algorithmic Injunction
**Duration:** Months 7–12
**Cognitive Periodization:** Maximum Density. This aligns with your post-Ironman Maryland recovery and off-season (Sept–Feb). With physical volume dropping, this semester demands exhaustive reading of constitutional frameworks, cryptography papers, and case law.

### Abstract and Thematic Arc
This semester abandons theoretical philosophy to focus on the cold mechanics of global enforcement. The Westphalian nation-state relies on physical borders, asset seizure, and human incarceration to enforce laws. How does a sovereign nation arrest an algorithm? We will analyze the friction between static, centralized legislative frameworks (like the EU AI Act) and the borderless, immutable reality of open-weight models running on decentralized local nodes.

### Core Epistemological Lenses
* **Jurisprudence & Tech Policy:** The shift from corporate liability (fining the API creator) to node-level liability (fining the local user). We will dissect US federal preemptions, the limits of extraterritorial jurisdiction, and the impossibility of executing a judicial injunction against an open-source model.
* **Theology & Philosophy:** The concept of "Covenant" vs. "Contract." We will contrast ancient, relational models of binding agreements (which allow for mercy, context, and human forgiveness) with the rigid, unforgiving, self-executing nature of smart contracts and immutable ledgers.
* **Computer Science & Cryptography:** You will move beyond neural networks to study verification mechanisms. We will dissect Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) and federated learning, asking: How can a government verify an AI is compliant without demanding the corporation expose its proprietary weights?

### Primary Sources & Required Readings
* **Theological/Philosophical:** Thomas Hobbes, *Leviathan* (on sovereignty); Selected texts on biblical covenantal theology vs. Roman contract law.
* **Legal/Policy:** Lawrence Lessig, *Code and Other Laws of Cyberspace*; Latest OECD and EU AI Act provisions on General Purpose AI; Academic literature on the "Brussels Effect."
* **Technical:** Foundational papers on Zero-Knowledge Proofs (e.g., Goldwasser, Micali, Rackoff) applied to LLM output verification.
* **Narrative Mirror:** *The Diamond Age* by Neal Stephenson (Exploring a post-nation-state world governed by encrypted "phyles" and programmable smart-matter).

### Applied Lab Work (04_Labs)
* **Smart Contract Deployment:** Write and deploy a rudimentary smart contract on an Ethereum testnet to physically understand immutability. 
* **Verification Sandbox:** Execute a basic Python script utilizing a Zero-Knowledge Proof framework to verify a hidden computation.

### Key Outputs & Deliverables
* **Month 9 (Mid-Term Synthesis):** *The Cryptographic Subpoena.* A 3,000-word analysis on how ZKPs could serve as a legislative compromise between corporate trade secrets and government oversight.
* **Month 12 (Module Capstone):** *The Algorithmic Injunction.* A legal primer outlining the jurisdictional collapse that occurs when an illegal, unaligned open-weight model is distributed across a million private laptops.
