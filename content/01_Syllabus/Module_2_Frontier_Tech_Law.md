# Module 2: Frontier Tech Law & The Sovereignty of Code

**Anticipated Training Phase:** Post-Ironman Maryland Recovery & Off-Season (Sept - Feb)
**Cognitive Load Allocation:** High. With physical volume dropping post-race, this semester requires heavy, dense reading of legal frameworks, case law, and Python execution.

### Thematic Focus
This semester abandons theoretical philosophy to focus on the cold mechanics of global enforcement. If Module 1 proved that AI agency is distributed, Module 2 asks: *How does a Westphalian nation-state arrest an algorithm?* We will analyze the friction between static sovereign borders and borderless, open-weight models. 

### Core Academic Intersections
* **Legal:** The shift from corporate liability (fining the API creator) to node-level liability (fining the local user). We will analyze the evolution of the EU AI Act and US federal preemptions.
* **Theological:** The concept of "Covenant" vs. "Contract." How do ancient models of binding agreements map onto immutable, self-executing smart contracts? 
* **Technical:** Cryptographic verification. You will learn the architecture of Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) and how they might be used to verify algorithmic compliance without exposing proprietary weights.

### Semester Project (M.Phil Primer)
A drafted legal framework addressing the impossibility of an algorithmic injunction. If an open-source model is deemed illegal, how do you wipe it from a decentralized network?
